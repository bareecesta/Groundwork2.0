About Groundwork

Groundwork for young baristas

We are a social enterprise that uses coffee as a pathway into meaningful employment for 16–24 year olds.

Why we exist

Too many young people with potential are locked out of hospitality careers. Groundwork changes that by offering barista training that feels like the job, not the classroom, backed up with mentors who have stood behind the machine.

We focus on young adults still in school, transitioning out of education or currently out of work. By connecting them with supportive employers, we help each trainee move from first shot to first shift.

“Our mission: to create routes into meaningful hospitality work for young people through barista training, mentoring and employer partnerships.”

How we work

Training with purpose

Every session blends specialty coffee standards with confidence-building exercises. Trainees leave with the technical skills cafés demand and the self-belief to thrive in service environments.

Mentoring that sticks

Our coaches stay close before, during and after placements. They help young people navigate expectations, communicate with managers and keep growing long after the training ends.

Employers that care

We partner with cafés and hospitality brands that see beyond the CV. Together, we shape placements that offer real responsibility and future hiring opportunities.

Support for schools

Schools, colleges and pupil referral units invite us in to run on-site programmes, building bridges between education and employment for their learners.
